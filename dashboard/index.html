A z-score of 6.77 is extreme — this is more than 6 standard deviations above normal. The bot would have flagged this immediately and sent alerts to both email and Slack. Worth investigating whether this was a one-off bulk order, a pricing error, or a genuine demand event.
You
Which 3 products have the biggest margin opportunity? And what would change if I optimised for revenue instead?
KA
Analytics Assistant
For margin: all three are Electronics, currently priced well below optimal.
Electronics 07: $138 → $215 (+56%) · margin uplift +102%
Electronics 02: $126 → $193 (+54%) · margin uplift +87%
Electronics 09: $443 → $612 (+38%) · margin uplift +87%
If you switch to revenue: the recommendations flip almost entirely. The model would suggest lowering these same products — Electronics 07 drops to ~$112, Electronics 02 to ~$99. Revenue uplift is smaller (+28–37%) but you'd capture significantly more volume.

This is the core pricing trade-off: margin optimisation raises prices and accepts lower volume; revenue optimisation does the opposite. Electronics is the most elastic category (−2.1), so the difference between objectives is especially sharp here.
